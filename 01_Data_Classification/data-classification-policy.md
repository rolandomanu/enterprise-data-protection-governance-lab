# Enterprise Data Classification Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a standardized data classification framework that supports data protection, regulatory compliance, risk management, and information governance across the organization.

## Classification Levels

### Public

Information approved for public disclosure.

Examples:
- Marketing materials
- Public website content
- Published reports

Requirements:
- No special handling required
- May be shared externally

---

### Internal

Information intended for internal business use.

Examples:
- Internal procedures
- Organizational charts
- Internal communications

Requirements:
- Access limited to employees and authorized personnel
- External sharing requires management approval

---

### Confidential

Sensitive business information that could impact operations if disclosed.

Examples:
- Customer information
- Financial reports
- Employee records
- Vendor contracts

Requirements:
- Encryption required during transmission
- Access based on business need
- Sharing restricted to authorized personnel

---

### Restricted

Highly sensitive information requiring the highest level of protection.

Examples:
- Social Security Numbers (SSN)
- Payment card information
- Authentication credentials
- Security investigation records
- Regulatory protected information

Requirements:
- Encryption required at rest and in transit
- Strict access control
- Logging and monitoring required
- Executive approval required for sharing

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Data Owners

Responsible for:
- Classifying information
- Approving access
- Reviewing classification periodically

### Employees

Responsible for:
- Handling information according to classification level
- Reporting suspected data exposure incidents

### Security Team

Responsible for:
- Monitoring compliance
- Supporting DLP controls
- Investigating policy violations

## Policy Review

This policy should be reviewed annually or whenever significant regulatory, operational, or technological changes occur.
